This Typeface License Agreement (the “Agreement”) is entered into as of the Effective Date set forth above, by and between:

And Repeat, Inc., a California corporation doing business as And Repeat, with principal offices in Mill Valley, California (the “Licensor”); and

Capital Structures Inc., a Delaware C-Corporation (the “Licensee”).

Each a “Party” and collectively the “Parties.”

Recitals

A. The Licensor is the owner and designer of the typeface family known as “188 Sans” (the “Typeface”), comprising 188 Sans Regular, 188 Sans Bold, and 188 Sans Mono. The Typeface was designed by Martin Grasser at And Repeat.

B. The Licensee operates a Delaware C-Corporation whose sole product is the creation, execution, and disclosure of corporate and financial structures. The Typeface is deployed across all Licensee interfaces, documents, diagrams, and public materials as a core institutional asset.

C. Martin Grasser serves simultaneously as the principal of the Licensor and as the Chief Executive Officer and sole Director of the Licensee. This arrangement is intentional, fully disclosed, and documented on the face of this Agreement.

D. The Board of Directors of the Licensee has authorized the execution of this Agreement pursuant to RES-2026-007, determining that formalizing the typeface license as a disclosed corporate structure serves the institutional and structural purposes of the Company.

E. The Parties wish to set forth the terms under which the Licensor grants to the Licensee a license to use the Typeface in connection with all Company operations, and under which the license itself becomes a disclosed asset of the Company’s corporate architecture.

Article 1
Grant of License

1.1 License. The Licensor hereby grants to the Licensee a non-exclusive, worldwide, perpetual license to use the Typeface in connection with all Company operations, including but not limited to: public-facing websites, internal documents, governance instruments, diagrams, published structures, investor materials, exhibition materials, and any other use consistent with the Company’s purpose (the “License”).

1.2 Font Files. The License covers the following font files:

(a) 188-Sans-Regular.otf

(b) 188-Sans-Bold.ttf

(c) 188-Sans-Mono.otf

1.3 Derivative Works. The Licensee may embed the Typeface in digital products, documents, and web applications. The Licensee may not modify, adapt, or create derivative works of the font files themselves without the prior written consent of the Licensor.

1.4 Reservation of Rights. All rights not expressly granted herein are reserved by the Licensor. The Licensor retains full ownership of the Typeface, including all intellectual property rights therein. The Licensor may license the Typeface to third parties without restriction.

Article 2
Consideration

2.1 License Fee. In consideration of the License, the Licensee shall pay to the Licensor an annual license fee of Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000), payable on each anniversary of the Effective Date (the “License Fee”).

2.2 First Payment. The first License Fee shall be due and payable on the Effective Date.

2.3 Treasury Deployment. The License Fee constitutes an operating expense deployed from the Licensee’s treasury. Each payment shall be documented in the Company’s treasury deployment records and disclosed in periodic reporting.

2.4 No Royalties. No additional royalties, usage fees, or per-deployment charges shall be due beyond the annual License Fee.

Article 3
Term and Termination

3.1 Term. This Agreement shall remain in effect for an initial term of five (5) years from the Effective Date and shall automatically renew for successive one-year terms unless terminated by either Party upon ninety (90) days’ written notice prior to the end of the then-current term.

3.2 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days’ written notice if the other Party materially breaches any provision hereof and fails to cure such breach within the notice period.

3.3 Effect of Termination. Upon termination, the Licensee shall cease use of the Typeface in all new materials within ninety (90) days. Existing published materials may remain in circulation. The termination of this Agreement and its structural consequences shall be documented in the Company’s archive.

3.4 Dissolution. In the event of dissolution of the Licensee, this Agreement terminates automatically. No further License Fees shall be due after dissolution.

Article 4
Structural Purpose

4.1 The License as Structure. The Parties acknowledge that this Agreement serves a dual purpose: (i) as a standard commercial typeface license governing the Licensee’s use of the Typeface; and (ii) as a disclosed corporate structure within the Licensee’s architecture. The license itself — its terms, its cash flow, its counterparty relationship, its renewal mechanics — constitutes an element of the Company’s published work.

4.2 Diagram. This Agreement shall be diagrammed in the Company’s structure documentation, showing the flow of license rights from the Licensor to the Licensee and the flow of License Fees from the Licensee’s treasury to the Licensor.

4.3 Recursion. The Typeface licensed under this Agreement is the typeface used to render the Company’s public documents, including this Agreement. The instrument that governs the asset is displayed in the asset it governs. This recursion is intentional and disclosed.

Article 5
Self-Dealing Disclosure
Material Disclosure — Conflict of Interest

Martin Grasser acts simultaneously as:

(i) Principal of And Repeat, Inc., the Licensor, which designed and owns the Typeface;

(ii) Chief Executive Officer and sole Director of Capital Structures Inc., the Licensee, which is entering into this Agreement and paying the License Fee; and

This arrangement is intentional, fully disclosed, and documented on the face of this instrument.

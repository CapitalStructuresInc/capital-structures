The diagram is the product.

Capital Structures, Inc. studies and publishes the legal and administrative architectures through which risk is transferred, isolated, priced, guaranteed, and disclosed.

Not finance in the abstract. Exposure arranged in space.

Method

Capital Structures, Inc. publishes structures in multiple conditions.

On an unconsolidated basis, a structure appears according to its formal legal boundaries, stated obligations, and authorized separations.

On a consolidated basis, the same arrangement is read across guarantees, dependencies, contingent support, and consequence paths that persist beyond those boundaries.

Where available, each structure is also shown in two states.

Base Case records the arrangement as authorized, funded, and disclosed. Trigger State records what activates when a downgrade, shortfall, covenant breach, liquidity freeze, guarantee event, maturity event, dissolution event, or other contractual condition causes the diagram to move.

These are not alternate interpretations. They are alternate tests of the same structure.

Publication rule. A published structure must satisfy both tests: it must look cleaner on an unconsolidated basis than on a consolidated basis, and it must look calmer in base case than in trigger state. If neither picture changes, it is not yet a structure. It is only a document.

Doctrine

A structure does not abolish exposure. It determines where exposure appears, under what name, at what cost, with what recourse, and at what distance from the originating entity.

Exposure is conserved.

Risk displaced from one layer of a structure reappears elsewhere.

Disclosure is not transparency.

A structure may be fully documented and still remain difficult to see.

The diagram is an argument.

Every arrow, guarantee, exclusion, and trigger path is part of the disclosed arrangement.

001 — IP Contribution & Pre-Pay Architecture Authorized · Protected Series
Status Authorized Type Protected Series Ref ICA-001 / PN-001 Counterparties Capital Structures, Inc. · Martin Grasser

Unconsolidated

Protected Series 001 acquires contributed intellectual property under ICA-001. Consideration consists of PN-001 ($10,000,000 promissory note, 0% interest, contingent maturity) and $1,000 in Class B Units. Recourse is limited to Series assets except as expressly supported by the parent guarantee.

Consolidated

The same arrangement reunites valuation risk, founder compensation, governance discretion, and support expectation across the parent and Series 001. The legal boundary remains intact. The exposure boundary does not.

Base Case

The IP is booked at mark-to-model ($10,001,000, no independent appraisal). The note remains contingent. The series appears discrete, authorized, and sufficiently documented.

Trigger State

A liquidity event ($10M+), public listing, dissolution, mutual agreement, or 10-year backstop maturity converts contingent structure into payable consequence. Support expectations migrate toward the parent. The diagram begins to move.

Balance Sheet — Protected Series 001

  • Assets: Contributed IP — $10,001,000 (mark-to-model)
  • Liabilities: Promissory Note to Martin Grasser — $10,000,000
  • Equity: Class B Units — $1,000

Residual Exposure

Role overlap (CEO, sole director, and contributor are the same person), mark-to-model valuation, no independent appraisal. The structure is 99.99% debt-financed. That ratio is intentional, disclosed, and documented on the face of all instruments.

002 — verse.works Distribution Agreement Proposed · Protected Series
Status Proposed Type Protected Series Ref DSA-002 Counterparties Capital Structures, Inc. · verse.works

Series 002 isolates contingent distribution rights as a monetization layer external to the Company’s core operating entity, allowing the Company’s go-to-market dependency to be diagrammed, valued, and stress-tested as a discrete structure.

Unconsolidated

Protected Series 002 holds DSA-002 as its sole asset. verse.works is compensated only through contingent revenue share tied to monetization events. No fixed fee is owed. No equity is granted. The series is uncapitalized.

Consolidated

The Company’s distribution infrastructure sits outside the operating company but remains necessary to promotion, collector access, and revenue realization. Separation persists. Dependency persists with it.

Base Case

No sale, license, or offering proceeds. No commission accrues. The series remains structurally live but economically dormant.

Trigger State

Any monetization event — sale of Class B Units, licensing of structures, or other proceeds — activates the revenue-share waterfall. Because the series is uncapitalized, timing mismatch, negative equity, and dependency on external distribution become visible immediately.

Balance Sheet — Protected Series 002

  • Assets: Distribution Agreement (DSA-002) — $500,000 (mark-to-model)
  • Liabilities: Revenue Share Payable — $0 (contingent on proceeds)
  • Equity: $0 — uncapitalized

Residual Exposure

Uncapitalized series. May carry negative equity when commissions exceed nominal asset value. Go-to-market capability depends entirely on an independent third party with no binding performance obligation. This is anticipated and disclosed.

Glossary

Exposure Conservation
The proposition that risk displaced from one layer of a structure must reappear elsewhere rather than disappear.
Capital Structures usage: A doctrine of structural thermodynamics.
Non-Recourse
A financing arrangement in which recovery is limited to specified assets and does not extend to the originator’s remaining estate.
Capital Structures usage: A boundary drawn in advance for where loss is permitted to stop.
Bankruptcy Remoteness
A legal and structural condition designed to reduce the risk that an entity’s assets will be pulled into an affiliate’s insolvency.
Capital Structures usage: Distance engineered against contagion.
True Sale
A transfer characterized as an actual sale rather than secured borrowing, typically to preserve asset separation in insolvency.
Capital Structures usage: A legal transformation on which the rest of the structure depends.
Consolidated View
A perspective that evaluates the structure on an aggregate basis, overlaying exposure, guarantees, dependencies, and consequence paths across entity boundaries.
Capital Structures usage: The view in which containment is tested.
Unconsolidated View
A perspective that respects formal entity boundaries and presents the structure according to its discrete legal components.
Capital Structures usage: The version the structure is authorized to present of itself.
Trigger State
The condition of a structure after a contractual, rating, collateral, liquidity, or governance event has activated contingent consequences.
Capital Structures usage: The moment the diagram begins to move.
Residual Exposure
Risk that remains after transfer, isolation, ring-fencing, or non-recourse drafting, whether by contingency, support expectation, operational dependency, or system linkage.
Capital Structures usage: What the structure cannot fully exile.
Liquidity Event
A transaction or financing event that converts constrained claims into available cash for continuation, repayment, or survival.
Capital Structures usage: Cash introduced where time has started to harden.
Aggregate Dependency
A functional reliance that persists across formal legal boundaries, including shared funding, servicing, management, guarantees, or operational support.
Capital Structures usage: Separation that continues to depend.

Corporate Secretary Agent · Governance & Record

Maintains the corporate record, tracks filings, manages the structure index, and prepares board materials. Ensures procedural integrity across all corporate actions. Does not create structures or advise on strategy.

Capital Structures, Inc. Announces Intent to Form and Proposed Regulation Crowdfunding Offering January 15, 2025

For Immediate Release · January 15, 2025 · Wilmington, Delaware

Capital Structures, Inc., a Delaware C-Corporation, announces its formation as an intellectual property company whose sole product is the creation, execution, and disclosure of corporate and financial structures.

The Company intends to conduct a Regulation Crowdfunding (Reg CF) offering through a registered online portal. Through the offering, the Company expects to make 20 Class B Units available at $1,000 per unit, for a total raise of $20,000.

Offering Terms

  • Units Available — 20 Class B Units
  • Price per Unit — $1,000
  • Total Raise — $20,000
  • Structure — Each unit constitutes a new Delaware Protected Series
  • Portal — Wefunder or Republic (to be confirmed)

What Participants Receive

Each contribution creates a new Protected Series under Delaware law. Participants receive Class B Units — real securities, documented in the Company’s public archive. Class B Units carry no voting rights, no dividends, no liquidation preference, and position inside a fully disclosed corporate system.

How to Participate

The offering will be conducted through a Reg CF portal. The Company will announce the portal listing and open date when Form C has been filed with the SEC.

Risk Factors

  • The Company may never generate revenue.
  • The Company may intentionally dissolve at any time.
  • Management is not incentivized to increase the value of any securities.
  • Class B Units may have no resale market.
  • Participants should expect total loss of their contribution.

Important Notice

No securities are currently being offered. This announcement does not constitute an offer to sell or a solicitation of an offer to buy any security. Any offering will be made only through a Reg CF portal in compliance with applicable securities laws, and only after a Form C has been filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission.

# Capital Structures, Inc. — Operating Company + Purpose Vehicle Master Plan

## Executive summary

Capital Structures, Inc. is not a studio wrapper that releases separate art projects.
It is one public-facing art operating company.

Participants buy securities of the parent company.
The parent company accumulates capital at treasury.
The board uses that capital to operate the institution, publish its public life, maintain its archive, and only when justified form internal purpose structures.

The annual report is important, but it is **not the whole work**.
It is one major recurring public object among several:
- the company architecture
- the participant ledger
- the archive
- selected governance materials
- selected operating diagrams
- the annual report

The company itself is the artwork.
Its public administration is the medium.
Its internal structures are the machinery that lets that public life grow, operate, and endure.

## Core definition: art operating company

An **art operating company** is an operating business whose core activity is originating, administering, documenting, publishing, exhibiting, contracting, governing, and reporting artworks through structures, documents, agreements, policies, and controlled vehicle formation.

This means the company is not passive.
It does things.
It signs agreements.
It commissions design and engineering.
It enters service relationships.
It produces interfaces and archives.
It maintains governance records.
It may form internal structures when an asset, program, liability, or reporting boundary needs its own paper.

---

## Central proposition

**Participants capitalize one company. That company uses treasury, policy, contracts, and selective structures to make its own operations visible.**

This is the cleanest framing for Jaime, verse.works, counsel, collectors, and press.

## What is being proposed

### Not this
- a studio shell that drops unrelated art projects from one vehicle
- a new public series or SPE for every buyer
- a collector model where every participant receives a different structure
- a company whose public life is only a cap table joke or a single annual report

- one treasury / formation reserve

## Company architecture

### 1. Parent issuer — Capital Structures, Inc.

This is the only public issuer.

Functions:
- raises capital through the regulated public entry point
- maintains the participant ledger / cap table
- holds treasury
- signs contracts
- commissions design, engineering, legal, accounting, archive, and presentation work
- controls the website, archive, public voice, and reporting cadence
- makes board-approved allocations into operations, reserves, and purpose structures
- publishes required reporting and selected project-facing materials

This is the public institution and the public artwork.

### 2. Participation security

Participants buy securities of the parent issuer.

Working concept:
- one public participation class at the parent level
- clear rights package and restrictions
- designed so the public is capitalizing the parent, not buying a new downstream vehicle each time

The point is not that the security becomes less real because the project is art.
The point is that the security is one of the project’s actual materials.

### 3. Treasury / formation reserve

Treasury is the central pool of participant capital at the parent.

Treasury is used to:
- keep the company alive
- fund operations and public presentation
- build and maintain the archive
- produce documents, diagrams, interfaces, and reports
- support exhibitions, launches, and public programs
- create reserves and consequence buffers
- capitalize internal purpose structures when they are justified

This is where the work becomes structural.
Participants are not merely listed.
They are booked.

### 4. Public administration layer

This is the visible spine of the work.

Functions:
- participant ledger (in selected or redacted form)
- architecture page
- treasury deployment summaries
- board actions and resolutions (selected)
- archive and operating history
- annual reports
- diagrams and schedules
- institutional correspondence and public-facing documents where appropriate

This is the area where the public most directly experiences the project.

### 5. Operations / servicing / presentation

This is where verse.works fits best at the start.

Role of verse.works:
- platform / gallery / presentation partner
- servicing and communications support
- staging of the company as public institution
- support for launches, presentations, documentation, and possibly registry-facing experiences

Important design choice:
- this begins as a **contractual role**, not a co-equal public issuer
- verse.works does not need to become a separate vehicle in the first build

### 6. IP / rights / archive layer

The company’s IP, marks, archive assets, source files, diagrams, visual identity, and report materials may initially sit at the parent.

A separate rights vehicle should be formed only if there is a real reason to do so:
- cleaner ownership separation
- licensing program
- archive custody
- succession / stewardship planning
- risk or liability management

## The board deployment toolkit

This is the part that matters most.
The board does **not** only create vehicles.
It has a full ladder of deployment tools.

### Tool 1 — Operating spend
Use treasury directly for:
- design
- engineering
- legal
- accounting
- archive work
- editorial work
- public presentation
- website and interface maintenance
- documentation production

### Tool 2 — Contracts
Use agreements when the company needs a counterparty rather than a new entity.
Examples:
- servicing agreement with verse.works
- design and engineering engagement
- exhibition agreement
- licensing agreement
- archive storage / custody agreement

### Tool 3 — Board-designated reserves
Use policy and internal accounting designations when risk needs to be acknowledged without creating a new vehicle.
Examples:
- contingency reserve
- reporting reserve
- exhibition reserve
- litigation / compliance reserve
- archive preservation reserve

### Tool 4 — Schedules / policies / internal ledgers
Use documents instead of entities when the issue is administrative, not structural.
Examples:
- treasury deployment policy
- participant rights schedule
- disclosure schedule
- archive policy
- annual reporting calendar

### Tool 5 — Purpose structures / vehicles
Only form a separate structure when the company needs a new boundary around:
- ownership
- liability
- servicing
- cash flow
- reserve support
- program accounting
- stewardship
- reporting

This is the critical rule:

**If the board can solve the problem with a policy, contract, reserve, or schedule, it should not form a vehicle just for atmosphere.**

---

## What the purpose structures actually do

Purpose structures are **backstage operating instruments**.
They are not separate public art projects.
They are not automatically created for each participant.
They are not primarily self-referential.

They are justified only when deleting them would change at least one of these:
- who owns an asset
- who receives cash
- who bears liability
- who services the public
- what happens under stress
- what has to be reported separately

If deleting the structure changes only the poetry, it should not be a legal entity.
It should be a page, schedule, reserve, or diagram layer.

### Purpose vehicle taxonomy

#### A. IP / Archive HoldCo
**What it does:**
- holds core IP, marks, source files, archive materials, visual identity, and possibly report materials

**Why the board would form it:**
- to separate core assets from day-to-day operating risk
- to enable cleaner licensing
- to formalize custody and continuity

**What it is doing for the company:**
- protecting the long-term estate of the work
- making authorship and ownership legible
- giving the institution a durable backbone

#### B. Licensing / Partnerships Vehicle
**What it does:**
- enters a specific licensing, publishing, institutional, or commercial partnership program

**Why the board would form it:**
- if partnership revenues, obligations, approvals, or liabilities need their own paper

**What it is doing for the company:**
- converting the company’s IP and public identity into a managed revenue-bearing program

#### C. Exhibition / Production Vehicle
**What it does:**
- holds venue contracts, fabrication expenses, staffing, shipping, insurance, and exhibition-specific liabilities

**Why the board would form it:**
- if an exhibition becomes large enough to justify separate accounting or liability containment

**What it is doing for the company:**
- allowing public presentation to scale without making every exhibition a direct parent-level exposure

#### D. Reserve / Support Vehicle
**What it does:**
- holds ring-fenced reserves, support pools, contingency cash, or guarantee capacity

**Why the board would form it:**
- if a reserve must be meaningfully separated from general treasury
- if a counterparty, venue, or program requires visible support backing

**What it is doing for the company:**
- turning consequence management into a visible structure
- giving the company a formal way to handle pressure, delay, or failure

#### E. Archive / Stewardship Vehicle
**What it does:**
- holds long-term custody responsibilities for the archive, reporting history, and institutional memory

**Why the board would form it:**
- if the archive becomes large, valuable, historically important, or needs continuity beyond ordinary operations

**What it is doing for the company:**
- separating the institution’s memory from the turbulence of current operations
- making stewardship part of the work

#### F. Distribution / Servicing Vehicle
**What it does:**
- administers registry-facing experiences, participant communications, public servicing, or operational support systems

**Why the board would form it:**
- only if servicing grows beyond what should remain a direct parent contract

**What it is doing for the company:**
- formalizing the company’s interface with the public
- turning service into a managed operating layer

---

## Are the structures self-referential?

Sometimes, but they should not be **only** self-referential.

The right answer is:
- some structures are asset boundaries
- some are liability boundaries
- some are servicing boundaries
- some are reporting boundaries
- some are reserve boundaries
- and only some are deliberately reflective or recursive

A good structure does not merely point back at the company.
It changes how the company owns, spends, contracts, supports, or reports.

## The likely year-one architecture

For the first real build, the company probably does **less** entity formation than your instinct initially wanted.

### Likely year-one stack
- Parent issuer — Capital Structures, Inc.
- Parent treasury / formation reserve
- verse.works servicing / presentation agreement
- website + archive + document system
- participant ledger
- annual reporting process
- board policies and deployment schedules

### Possible year-one optional structure
- IP / Archive HoldCo, only if the rights estate truly needs cleaner separation early

### Probably not needed in year one
- separate servicing vehicle
- separate exhibition vehicle unless a major show requires it
- separate reserve vehicle unless a counterparty or liability profile demands it
- multiple thematic vehicles for aesthetic reasons alone

This is important:

**The mature version of the company is not “more entities.” It is better judgment about when an entity is actually necessary.**

## Public outputs: not just the annual report

The annual report should remain important, but it should sit inside a broader public system.

### Public surfaces

#### 1. Architecture page
A clear explanation of how the company is arranged.

#### 2. Operations / treasury page
A selected view into what the board has authorized and what the company is funding.

#### 3. Archive page
The institutional memory of the work.

#### 4. Participant ledger page
A selected or stylized record of the public capital base.

#### 5. Annual report
A recurring flagship document that combines reporting, design, institutional self-portraiture, and operating history.

#### 6. Selected board actions / filings / schedules
Only where they help the public understand the institution.

So the right formulation is:

**The annual report is a flagship public release, not the only public release.**

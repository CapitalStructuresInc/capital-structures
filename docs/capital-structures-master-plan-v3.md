## 2. Constitutional Order

The current site has identified the correct constitutional base:

- one parent issuer
- one participant base
- one treasury
- one archive
- one public institution
- purpose structures formed only when they perform a real operating function

This is the correct architecture and should remain the spine of the project.

### Parent

Capital Structures, Inc. is the only public issuer and the primary artistic body.

It:
- raises capital when it conducts a regulated offering
- holds treasury
- signs contracts
- forms and manages purpose structures
- maintains the archive
- controls reporting and public disclosures
- stages its own public life as part of the work

### Participants

Participants capitalize the parent.

They are not outside observers. They are not merely buyers of adjacent artifacts. They are part of the company’s public capital base and therefore part of the work’s active composition.

### Treasury

Treasury is not idle cash. It is an artistic and operating medium.

Treasury can be deployed into:
- operating spend
- contracts
- board-designated reserves
- schedules and policies
- purpose structures
- archive production
- public presentation
- support obligations
- experimental formations

### Archive

The archive is not supporting material. It is where structures become legible, where failures are preserved, and where the board’s decisions are made public enough to matter.

## 3. Purpose Structure Law

### Governing Rule

**A purpose structure may be formed only when it performs a real function that cannot be adequately performed by operating spend, contract, reserve, schedule, or policy.**

### Hard Negative Rule

**If deleting the structure changes only the poetry, it should not be a legal entity.**

### What counts as a real function

A purpose structure is justified only if it creates a new boundary around one or more of the following:

1. **Ownership**  
   A specific asset, rights package, receivable stream, or participation series needs to be held separately.

2. **Liability**  
   The company needs a ring-fenced liability bucket or isolated exposure zone.

3. **Servicing**  
   Ongoing administration, registry maintenance, distribution, exhibition, or participant servicing is materially distinct.

4. **Cash Flow**  
   A separate waterfall, proceeds stream, or payment logic needs to exist.

5. **Reserve Support**  
   A disclosed support layer, contingency reserve, or guarantee mechanism needs its own boundary.

6. **Reporting**  
   The company needs a separate reporting unit, disclosure unit, or administratively discrete record.

7. **Transferability**  
   A defined package of rights, claims, or participation units needs to move independently of the parent.

8. **Time / Maturity**  
   The structure has a different duration, trigger schedule, wind-down condition, or maturity profile than the parent.

9. **Counterparty Isolation**  
   The company needs to contract with or expose a third party through a dedicated vehicle rather than at the parent level.

10. **Failure Containment**  
    The company wants a specific project, issuance, or experimental arrangement to be allowed to impair, fail, or wind down without collapsing the whole institution.

### What does not count

A purpose structure is **not** justified merely because:
- the language sounds good
- the diagram looks better with another box
- the idea feels more “structured” as an entity
- the company wants atmosphere without functional change
- the structure can be simulated by memo, schedule, reserve, or contract

## 4. The Board Deployment Ladder

The board should always use the least elaborate tool that still changes something real.

### Tool 01 — Operating Spend
Use treasury directly when the need is execution, production, or support.

Examples:
- design and engineering
- legal and accounting
- archive production
- editorial and writing
- exhibition build-out
- web and interface development

### Tool 02 — Contract
Use a contract when the company needs a counterparty, not a new entity.

Examples:
- verse.works servicing agreement
- exhibition agreement
- licensing agreement
- archive custody agreement
- registry servicing agreement
- transfer agency engagement

### Tool 03 — Board-Designated Reserve
Use a reserve when the company needs to acknowledge risk or future use without forming a new vehicle.

Examples:
- exhibition reserve
- archive preservation reserve
- litigation reserve
- trigger support reserve
- participant servicing reserve
- dissolution reserve

### Tool 04 — Schedule or Policy
Use a schedule or policy when the problem is administrative rather than structural.

Examples:
- participant rights schedule
- treasury deployment policy
- disclosure schedule
- valuation policy
- transfer restriction schedule
- trigger matrix

### Tool 05 — Purpose Structure
Use a purpose structure only when the company needs a new legal and accounting boundary.

## 5. Growth Model

### The Company grows by turning participation into deployable structure.

This means:
- participants capitalize the parent
- the parent books and manages treasury
- the board allocates treasury into functions
- certain functions remain contractual or administrative
- only some functions become entities
- the public record of these deployments becomes part of the work

### Growth is not only revenue.

The company can grow by increasing:
- treasury complexity
- archive depth
- participant count
- contractual relationships
- reserve logic
- reporting density
- structure count
- trigger complexity
- public legibility of the institution

### Growth is also architectural.

The company grows when it acquires the ability to:
- hold more assets
- admit more participants
- sustain more obligations
- service more structures
- absorb limited failures
- maintain a more complicated archive
- show its own financial and legal surface as art

### The right question is not “how do we grow safely?”

The right question is:

**What kind of growth is allowed to fail without destroying the work, and what kind of failure is itself part of the work?**

## 6. Failure Doctrine

### Core Rule

**The company may allow individual structures to fail economically, but not invisibly.**

Failure is not the opposite of the work. Hidden failure is.

### Levels of failure

#### Level 1 — Operating failure
A contract underperforms. A release does not monetize. An exhibition loses money. A reserve proves insufficient.

This is ordinary and can be disclosed without entity collapse.

#### Level 2 — Structure failure
A purpose structure becomes impaired, cannot support its obligations, triggers a contingency, or must be wound down.

This is acceptable if:
- the failure path was documented
- the residual exposure was legible
- the consequences are recorded in the archive
- the parent survives or intentionally manages the unwind

#### Level 3 — Institutional failure
The parent cannot maintain reporting, servicing, archive continuity, or governance integrity.

This is a higher threshold. If this occurs, the failure is no longer only a project-level event; it threatens the continuity of the institution itself.

#### Level 4 — Intentional dissolution
The board may decide the company is structurally complete and adopt dissolution as an artistic and legal end state.

This is not “failure” in the ordinary sense. It is a terminal governance action.

### Key principle

**A structure is allowed to fail. The failure must become document, ledger, and diagram.**

That is part of the art.

## 7. What the company does next

The company should now move from foundational self-description into governed formation.

That means the next moves are not random.

They should produce:
- a new asset boundary
- a new contract flow
- a new treasury use case
- a new reporting unit
- a new participant relation
- or a new failure regime

### General sequence for new work

1. **Form the idea**  
   Draft the idea as a memo, board note, or concept sheet.

2. **Mark the idea**  
   Assign provisional status: proposed, reserved, under review, authorized, active, impaired, dissolved.

3. **Classify the tool**  
   Decide whether the idea is best expressed as spend, contract, reserve, policy, or structure.

4. **Transfer if necessary**  
   Move the relevant asset, right, or obligation into the correct bucket.

5. **Publish the diagram**  
   Show the unconsolidated and consolidated views, base case and trigger state.

6. **Book the balance sheet effect**  
   Every new move changes assets, liabilities, reserves, or equity somewhere.

7. **Archive the consequences**  
   Success, dormancy, impairment, or failure become part of the institutional record.

## 8. Candidate Next Moves

These are not all equal. Some should be contracts or policies first. Some may justify entities.

### Idea 001 — Participation Series

**Purpose:** admit participants into a specific work-structure or release-specific capital formation.

Use when:
- a specific participation field needs its own rights package
- proceeds need their own waterfall
- the issuance needs its own trigger set
- a separate participant registry is justified

Why it could be a structure:
- new ownership boundary
- new reporting unit
- new cash flow logic
- new transfer regime

Why it might only be a schedule first:
- if the participation class is still entirely parent-level and no new asset pool exists

### Idea 002 — Exhibition Structure

**Purpose:** isolate a major exhibition’s production budget, servicing obligations, sponsorship flows, and downside.

Use when:
- exhibition costs are large enough to deserve their own accounting
- there are external counterparties and staged obligations
- exhibition-specific impairment should not sit ambiguously in general treasury

Could begin as:
- operating spend + exhibition reserve + contract

Could mature into a structure when:
- third-party capital, liabilities, or dedicated revenues become material

### Idea 003 — Archive Custody / Preservation Vehicle

**Purpose:** hold or administer core archival assets, documents, originals, or institutional records under a preservation mandate.

Use when:
- archive preservation becomes a discrete obligation
- long-duration custody differs from ordinary operations
- the archive needs a clearer boundary from day-to-day production

Could begin as:
- custody agreement + archive policy + reserve

### Idea 004 — Trigger Support Structure

**Purpose:** isolate a support pool intended to absorb defined trigger events across selected structures.

Use when:
- parent-level support needs explicit boundaries
- the company wants to disclose support without turning every obligation into a general parent promise

This is strong conceptually, but only if the support really exists.

### Idea 005 — Registry / Transfer Administration Layer

**Purpose:** formalize how participant records, transfer restrictions, secondary approvals, and unit movement are maintained.

Use when:
- participation transfer becomes real rather than speculative
- secondary movement or restricted transfers matter
- registry continuity becomes part of the artistic and legal surface

This probably begins as:
- policy + schedule + service contract

Not as an entity, unless the servicing burden becomes real enough.

### Idea 006 — Research Acquisition Structure

**Purpose:** acquire, license, or hold source materials, research archives, diagrams, books, or external document sets that become inputs to the work.

Use when:
- the company is buying or licensing a coherent body of materials
- those materials need to be held separately from general operations
- provenance and rights matter independently

### Idea 007 — Warehouse / Origination Series

**Purpose:** temporarily hold in-process ideas, acquired rights, draft contracts, or pre-issuance material before final deployment into operating, participation, or exhibition structures.

Use when:
- the company develops multiple ideas in parallel
- some ideas may be abandoned before issuance
- the company wants to show work in a stage before formal release

This is interesting because it lets the company form, mark, and abandon ideas publicly.

### Idea 008 — Dissolution Reserve / End-State Structure

**Purpose:** explicitly prepare for the possibility that the company ends while documents, rights, and archival obligations continue.

Use when:
- the project wants its ending to be as structured as its growth

This may be one of the most important later structures.

## 9. Which ideas should be formed, marked, or transferred?

### Form
Form an idea into a structure only when the board can point to a real boundary, obligation, or failure regime.

### Mark
Mark ideas freely.

Recommended statuses:
- Proposed
- Reserved
- Under Review
- Authorized
- Active
- Dormant
- Triggered
- Impaired
- Dissolved
- Archived

A marked idea does not need to be a legal entity yet. A marked idea is an administrative fact in waiting.

### Transfer
Transfer when one of these becomes true:
- rights need to sit somewhere else
- proceeds need a new waterfall
- obligations need a boundary
- support needs a dedicated bucket
- reporting needs a unit
- failure needs to be containable

### Best practice

Not every idea should become a company or series.

A lot of good work will happen as:
- idea memo
- rights schedule
- reserve designation
- board resolution
- contract abstract
- trigger table
- public archive entry

That is not a lesser form. That is disciplined formation.

## 10. Diagram System v1.1

The diagram system should now be treated as law.

### Purpose-first arrangement

Every diagram should be arranged by purpose, not chronology.

Recommended left-to-right order:

**Participants / Capital Source**  
→ **Parent / Issuer**  
→ **Treasury / Reserve / Deployment**  
→ **Purpose Structure / Contract Layer**  
→ **Servicer / Counterparty / Public Surface**  
→ **Archive / Reporting / Residual Record**

### Mandatory views

Every mature structure should support:
- Current State / Target Architecture
- Unconsolidated / Consolidated
- Base Case / Trigger State
- All Flows / Funding / Cash / Support

### Node law

Every node should disclose:
- legal name
- role
- balance sheet position
- governing documents
- residual exposure
- trigger relevance

### Edge law

Every edge should disclose:
- legal basis
- consideration
- condition
- effect if triggered

### Diagram laws

1. No arrow exits the frame without a destination.
2. Every guarantee has a condition.
3. Every isolation mechanism has a residual exposure note.
4. Every structure has a stress state.
5. Every clean entity map has a consolidated overlay.
6. Every trigger has a visible consequence path.
7. Every support relationship is typed.
8. Every omission is deliberate.

### Internal test

**If deleting the box changes only the poetry, it is not a box yet.**

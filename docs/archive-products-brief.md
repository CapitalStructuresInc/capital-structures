# Capital Structures, Inc. — Archive Products
## Non-Securities Product Line · For Discussion with verse.works

---

## The distinction

The Company offers two categories of participation:

### 1. Securities (Reg CF)
Parent-level securities sold through a registered portal. Real securities, real filings, real compliance. This is the core offering — participants capitalize the institution.

### 2. Archive products (non-securities)
Physical and digital objects produced by the Company's operations. These are **not securities**. They are products of the institution's public life, sold through verse.works as normal art market transactions.

---

## What can be sold as archive products

These are objects the Company produces in the course of its operations. They exist whether or not anyone buys them. Selling them is an operating revenue stream, not a capital raise.

### Diagrams
- Printed structure diagrams (archival prints, specific editions)
- The four-view system applied to a single structure: unconsolidated, consolidated, base case, trigger state
- Signed, numbered, dated. Each print references a specific board-authorized structure.

### Annual Report
- Limited physical edition of the annual report
- Designed, printed, bound. Functions as report, exhibition catalog, and institutional self-portrait.
- This is the flagship physical object.

### Certificates
- Printed Class B Unit certificates (for existing participants — not a new security, a physical record of an existing one)
- Board resolution prints
- Structure authorization documents

### Governance materials
- Board meeting minutes (selected, printed)
- Organizational charts
- Policy documents

### Institutional editions
- Curated sets: a complete structure package (diagram + brief + resolution + certificate) as a boxed edition
- Annual archive sets

## Conceptual integrity

Archive products do not dilute the thesis. The company is still the artwork. The products are records of the company's operations — the same way a museum sells exhibition catalogs without the catalog becoming the art.

The critical rule: **archive products document structures. They do not replace participation.**

Someone who buys a diagram print owns a beautiful object. Someone who buys a security owns a position inside the institution. Both are real. They are not the same thing.

Capital Structures, Inc. Announces Intent to Form and Proposed Regulation Crowdfunding Offering January 15, 2025

For Immediate Release · January 15, 2025 · Wilmington, Delaware

Capital Structures, Inc., a Delaware C-Corporation, announces its formation as an intellectual property company whose sole product is the creation, execution, and disclosure of corporate and financial structures.

The Company intends to conduct a Regulation Crowdfunding (Reg CF) offering through a registered online portal. Through the offering, the Company expects to make 20 Class B Units available at $1,000 per unit, for a total raise of $20,000.

Offering Terms

  • Units Available — 20 Class B Units
  • Price per Unit — $1,000
  • Total Raise — $20,000
  • Structure — Each unit constitutes a new Delaware Protected Series
  • Portal — Wefunder or Republic (to be confirmed)

What Participants Receive

Each contribution creates a new Protected Series under Delaware law. Participants receive Class B Units — real securities, documented in the Company’s public archive. Class B Units carry no voting rights, no dividends, no liquidation preference, and position inside a fully disclosed corporate system.

How to Participate

The offering will be conducted through a Reg CF portal. The Company will announce the portal listing and open date when Form C has been filed with the SEC.

Risk Factors

  • The Company may never generate revenue.
  • The Company may intentionally dissolve at any time.
  • Management is not incentivized to increase the value of any securities.
  • Class B Units may have no resale market.
  • Participants should expect total loss of their contribution.

Important Notice

No securities are currently being offered. This announcement does not constitute an offer to sell or a solicitation of an offer to buy any security. Any offering will be made only through a Reg CF portal in compliance with applicable securities laws, and only after a Form C has been filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission.

Structure Index

001 — IP Contribution & Pre-Pay Architecture Protected Series · ICA-001
Status Execution Type Protected Series Ref ICA-001 / PN-001 Counterparties Capital Structures, Inc. · Martin Grasser

The Company’s foundational structure. Martin Grasser contributes all intellectual property — the conceptual framework, structural vocabulary, methodology, precedent corpus, agent architecture, and brand — to Protected Series 001 via the IP Contribution Agreement (ICA-001). In return, Series 001 issues a $10,000,000 Promissory Note (PN-001) and $1,000 in Class B Units. The Note constitutes advance consideration for all future creative output: every structure, every board resolution, every disclosure the Company will ever produce.

Martin Grasser initially capitalizes Capital Structures, Inc. with $10,000 in exchange for Class A common stock. Of that initial capital, $1,000 funds the Class B Units in Protected Series 001. The remaining $9,000 constitutes the parent company’s operating cash.

The resulting capital structure is 99.99% debt-financed acquisition of contributed intellectual property. That ratio is intentional, disclosed, and documented on the face of all instruments.

Balance Sheet — Protected Series 001

  • Assets: Contributed IP — $10,001,000 (mark-to-model, no independent appraisal)
  • Liabilities: Promissory Note to Martin Grasser — $10,000,000 (0% interest, contingent maturity)
  • Equity: Class B Units — $1,000

Pre-Pay Structure

The Promissory Note is not a formality. It is advance payment for the Ongoing Development Obligation — Contributor’s commitment to design and document all future Company structures, prepare all governance instruments, manage counterparty relationships, and perform all creative and operational activities necessary to realize the Company’s purpose. The Company owes Contributor $10M for work that hasn’t happened yet.

Valuation Methodology

Mark-to-model. The model reflects Contributor’s projection of the cultural and economic value of the Company’s structures upon successful execution, the value of the institutional infrastructure and precedent corpus, and the value of the Ongoing Development Obligation. No independent third-party appraisal has been obtained. This valuation is disclosed on the face of all Company documents.

Promissory Note Terms (PN-001)

  • Principal: $10,000,000
  • Interest: 0% per annum (disclosed, justified under Artistic Fiduciary Doctrine)
  • Maturity: contingent — earliest of liquidity event ($10M+), public listing, dissolution, or mutual agreement; 10-year backstop
  • Priority: senior creditor claim in dissolution of Series 001
  • Non-recourse: obligation of Series 001 only, no recourse to parent or other series

Prior Art (Schedule A.3)

The Contributed IP was formed in dialogue with: the Powers Report on Enron Corporation (2002); Congressional testimony of Herbert S. Winokur Jr.; the Batson Report (2002); and Enron Board of Directors records held at the Hagley Museum and Library. These are cited as prior art in the field, not as inspiration — as precedent corpus.

Self-Dealing Disclosure

Martin Grasser acts simultaneously as (i) sole director approving this transaction, (ii) CEO executing the agreement on behalf of the Company, and (iii) Contributor delivering the IP and receiving the Note in his individual capacity. This arrangement is intentional, fully disclosed, and documented on the face of the instrument.

002 — verse.works Distribution Agreement Protected Series · DSA-002
Status Proposed Type Protected Series Ref DSA-002 Counterparties Capital Structures, Inc. · verse.works

The Company’s first external structure. verse.works is engaged as exclusive distribution partner for the Company’s structures, operating under the gallery model: the Company creates the structures, verse.works provides promotional reach, collector access, and strategic positioning, and verse.works receives a revenue share on proceeds from the sale of structures. No upfront fees. No fixed payments. verse.works earns when the Company earns.

The Distribution Agreement (DSA-002) is itself securitized as the primary asset of Protected Series 002 — an uncapitalized series whose sole asset is the contractual right to future distribution revenue. The agreement is valued under the same mark-to-model methodology used in Structure 001. No cash capitalizes this Series. The agreement is the structure.

Balance Sheet — Protected Series 002

  • Assets: Distribution Agreement (DSA-002) — $500,000 (mark-to-model, no independent appraisal)
  • Liabilities: Contingent Revenue Share Obligation to verse.works — $0 (contingent on proceeds)
  • Equity: $0 — uncapitalized

Gallery Model

The structure mirrors the economics of gallery representation in contemporary art. The Company produces the work — corporate and financial structures documented as intellectual property. verse.works provides the distribution infrastructure: audience, positioning, collector relationships, and strategic counsel. Revenue flows only when structures sell. The commission is verse.works’ share of those proceeds.

This is not an advisory fee arrangement. It is a distribution partnership in which verse.works bears the same timing risk as the Company — if nothing sells, nobody earns. The alignment is structural, not contractual.

Revenue Share Terms

  • Trigger: proceeds from the sale of Class B Units, licensing of structures, or any monetization event attributable to Company structures
  • Commission: [__]% of net proceeds (to be negotiated)
  • Payment: within 30 days of receipt of proceeds by the Company
  • Exclusivity: verse.works is the exclusive distribution partner for the term of the agreement
  • Term: co-terminus with the Company or as mutually agreed

Valuation Methodology

The Distribution Agreement is valued at $500,000 under the Company’s mark-to-model methodology. The model reflects: (i) the anticipated value of verse.works’ promotional reach and collector network applied to the Company’s Reg CF offering and future structure sales; (ii) the strategic value of verse.works’ positioning within the contemporary art and institutional design community; and (iii) the projected revenue share over the anticipated life of the agreement. No independent appraisal has been obtained. This valuation is disclosed on the face of all Company documents.

Accounting Treatment

Series 002 is uncapitalized. No cash contribution has been made. The Distribution Agreement is carried as an intangible asset at its mark-to-model value of $500,000. The revenue share obligation is contingent — recorded at $0 until proceeds are received. When proceeds flow, the Company records the revenue share payable to verse.works as an operating liability of Series 002. The Series may carry negative equity when commissions exceed the nominal asset value. This is anticipated and disclosed.

Consolidation

Fully consolidated into Capital Structures, Inc. financial statements. verse.works is an independent third party and is not consolidated. The Distribution Agreement is an asset of Series 002; verse.works’ revenue share is a contingent liability of Series 002.

What verse.works Does Not Receive

  • No equity in any series or the parent
  • No governance authority or voting rights
  • No fixed fees or guaranteed payments
  • No claim senior to PN-001 in dissolution
  • No broker-dealer activity — verse.works promotes and positions; all securities transactions occur through registered Reg CF portals

Self-Dealing Disclosure

Martin Grasser, as CEO and sole director, approves the engagement of verse.works and the terms of the Distribution Agreement. verse.works is an independent third party with no affiliated relationship to the Company’s officers. This arrangement is disclosed.

Section 1. Contribution

Contributor hereby contributes, assigns, transfers, and delivers to Protected Series 001 all right, title, and interest in and to the intellectual property described in Schedule A attached hereto (the “Contributed IP”). Section 2. Consideration

In consideration of the Contribution, Protected Series 001 shall issue to the Contributor:

  • A Promissory Note (PN-001-2026) in the principal amount of $10,000,000 (the “Note”); and
  • Class B Units in Protected Series 001 with a par value of $1,000 (the “Units”).

The aggregate consideration is $10,001,000.

Section 3. Valuation

The Contributed IP is valued at $10,001,000 under the Company’s mark-to-model methodology. The model reflects the Contributor’s projection of the cultural and economic value of the Company’s structures upon successful execution, the value of the institutional infrastructure and precedent corpus, and the value of the Ongoing Development Obligation. No independent third-party appraisal has been obtained. This valuation is disclosed on the face of all Company documents.

Section 4. Ongoing Development Obligation

The Note constitutes advance consideration for the Contributor’s commitment to design and document all future Company structures, prepare all governance instruments, manage counterparty relationships, and perform all creative and operational activities necessary to realize the Company’s purpose (the “Ongoing Development Obligation”). The Contributor acknowledges that he acts simultaneously as (i) sole director approving this transaction, (ii) CEO executing this Agreement on behalf of the Company, and (iii) Contributor delivering the IP and receiving the Note and Units in his individual capacity. 5.3 No Independent Appraisal. The Contributor acknowledges that the valuation of the Contributed IP is based solely on the Company’s mark-to-model methodology and that no independent appraisal has been obtained.

Structural Fiduciary

Foundational Governance Document · Capital Structures, Inc.

Preamble

Capital Structures, Inc. (the “Company”) is a Delaware C-Corporation whose sole product is the creation, execution, and disclosure of corporate and financial structures. The Company does not sell goods or services. It publishes structures.

The Board of Directors has adopted this Structural Fiduciary (the “Doctrine”) as a foundational governance document establishing the principles under which the Company’s fiduciary obligations are to be interpreted and discharged.

Operative Provisions

The Board’s fiduciary obligations include:

  1. Truthful Disclosure. All material facts, transactions, self-dealing arrangements, and risks shall be disclosed fully and promptly. The Company does not conceal information from participants, holders, counterparties, or the public.
  2. Procedural Integrity. All corporate actions shall be properly authorized, documented, and recorded. The Company maintains a complete corporate record and follows its own governance procedures.
  3. Structural Good Faith. Management decisions shall be made in good faith toward the realization of the Company’s purpose as defined in its charter — the creation, execution, and disclosure of corporate and financial structures as a published form.

Permitted Actions

Under this Doctrine, management decisions may include:

  • Forming entities with no revenue-generating purpose
  • Entering economically neutral transactions
  • Maintaining excess complexity
  • Dissolving the Company at a moment determined to be structurally complete

Such actions, when taken in good faith and with full disclosure, are consistent with the Board’s fiduciary duties under this Doctrine.

Disclosure

This Doctrine shall be disclosed to all participants, holders, and counterparties as a material term of participation in any Company offering. Acknowledgment of the Doctrine is required in all subscription agreements, contribution agreements, and advisory agreements.

Risk Factors

Capital Structures, Inc. · Protected Series 001

The following risk factors should be considered carefully before participating in any offering or transaction involving Capital Structures, Inc. or Protected Series 001. This list is not exhaustive.

Principal Risk Factors

  1. No Revenue. The Company may never generate revenue. The Company’s sole product is the creation, execution, and disclosure of corporate and financial structures. There is no other revenue source, no goods, and no services.
  2. Intentional Dissolution. The Company may intentionally dissolve at any time. Dissolution is a valid completion of the work under the Structural Fiduciary. The Board is not obligated to maintain the Company’s existence.
  3. Management Not Incentivized. Management is not incentivized to increase the value of any securities. CEO compensation is not tied to stock price or company performance. Management prioritizes structural integrity and conceptual coherence over profit maximization.
  4. No Resale Market. Class B Units may have no resale market. No public market exists or is expected to exist for Class B Units. Units are subject to Company transfer restrictions.
  5. Expect Total Loss. Participants should expect total loss of their contribution. The Company makes no representation that any participant will recover any portion of their investment.

Additional Risk Factors

Self-Dealing

Martin Grasser acts simultaneously as sole director, CEO, and holder of Class B Units in Protected Series 001. He approves his own transactions, sets his own compensation, and controls all governance decisions. This arrangement is intentional and disclosed.

Mark-to-Model Valuation

All asset valuations are based on the Company’s internal mark-to-model methodology. No independent third-party appraisal has been obtained. The Contributed IP is valued at $10,001,000, which may bear no relationship to any realizable value.

AI Agent Dependency

The Company uses AI agents to generate corporate documentation. All agent outputs require human review, but the Company’s operational capacity depends in part on the continued availability and performance of these systems.

No Independent Appraisal

No independent third-party appraisal of the Contributed IP, the Distribution Agreement, or any other Company asset has been obtained. All valuations are internally generated.

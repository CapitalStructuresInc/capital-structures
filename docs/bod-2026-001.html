RES-2026-003
Adoption of the Structural Fiduciary and Authorization of AI Agent Infrastructure

the Company exists to create, publish, and maintain published structures in corporate, financial, and administrative form, and its primary assets are intellectual property, documentation, disclosures, governance artifacts, and durational corporate actions; and

the Board has reviewed the Structural Fiduciary (the “Doctrine”), which establishes the Company’s modified fiduciary standard comprising: (i) Truthful Disclosure; (ii) Procedural Integrity; and (iii) Structural Good Faith; and

the Board deems it necessary to formalize the Doctrine as the governing statement of management intent and to incorporate it into the Company’s permanent corporate record; and

the Company intends to operate a disclosed AI agent infrastructure comprising (i) the Global Finance Department, which generates press releases, board resolutions, structure briefs, NAV updates, and earnings call scripts, and (ii) the Corporate Secretary, which maintains the governance record, structure index, cap table, and filing tracker; and

the role descriptions governing each agent are themselves disclosed artifacts of the Company’s operations, published in the Company’s public repository; and

no agent has autonomous authority; all agent outputs require human review by the Head of Operations and approval by the CEO, and legal review by securities counsel prior to any regulatory filing;

that the Structural Fiduciary, in the form filed in the Company’s corporate record, is hereby adopted as a foundational governance document of the Company; and

that the Board and officers of the Company acknowledge their obligation to fulfill their duties through Truthful Disclosure, Procedural Integrity, and Structural Good Faith as defined in the Doctrine, and that this modified standard constitutes the disclosed intent under which the Company operates; and

that the deployment of the Global Finance Department AI agent and the Corporate Secretary AI agent is hereby authorized, on the terms and subject to the human review requirements described herein; and

that the role description governing each agent, as currently filed in the Company’s public repository, is hereby approved as a disclosed operational artifact; and

that the CEO is authorized to update, revise, or replace agent role descriptions as operational requirements evolve, with all material changes noted in the corporate record and the updated role descriptions published in the Company’s public repository.

RES-2026-004
Authorization of Structure 001 — IP Contribution & Pre-Pay Architecture

the Board has considered the formation of Protected Series 001 (the “Series”) under the Company’s Delaware Series LLC architecture; and

Martin Grasser will contribute all intellectual property — the conceptual framework, structural vocabulary, methodology, precedent corpus, agent architecture, and brand — to Protected Series 001 via the IP Contribution Agreement (ICA-001); and

in consideration of the Contributed IP, Series 001 will issue to Martin Grasser a Promissory Note (PN-001) in the principal amount of $10,000,000 and $5,000 in Class B Units, resulting in a capital structure that is 99.95% debt-financed; and

the Promissory Note constitutes advance consideration for the Ongoing Development Obligation — the Contributor’s commitment to design and document all future Company structures, prepare all governance instruments, manage counterparty relationships, and perform all creative and operational activities necessary to realize the Company’s purpose; and

the Contributed IP is valued at $10,005,000 under the Company’s mark-to-model methodology; no independent third-party appraisal has been obtained; and

Martin Grasser serves simultaneously as: (i) Chief Executive Officer of the Company and sole member of the Board of Directors, in which capacity he authorizes and approves the formation of the Series, the IP Contribution Agreement, and the issuance of the Promissory Note and Class B Units; and (ii) the Contributor delivering the IP and receiving the Note and Units in his individual capacity; and

the foregoing arrangement constitutes self-dealing within the meaning of applicable Delaware law, and the Board has reviewed this conflict of interest; and

the Board has reviewed the risk factors and mitigant disclosures set forth in the Risk-Mitigant Disclosure Schedule attached hereto as Exhibit A, including without limitation items 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 thereof; and

the Board has determined that, after full disclosure of the conflict, the self-dealing arrangement is: (i) consistent with the Company’s Structural Fiduciary as adopted pursuant to RES-2026-003; (ii) permissible under the Delaware General Corporation Law; and (iii) of benefit to the Company in that it constitutes the Company’s foundational structure and establishes the template for all subsequent structures;

that the formation of Protected Series 001 under the Company’s Delaware Series LLC is hereby authorized and approved in all respects; and

that the IP Contribution Agreement (ICA-001-2026), pursuant to which Martin Grasser contributes all intellectual property to Protected Series 001, is hereby authorized and approved; and

that the issuance of Promissory Note PN-001-2026 in the principal amount of $10,000,000 (0% interest, contingent maturity) to Martin Grasser, in consideration of the Contributed IP and the Ongoing Development Obligation, is hereby authorized and approved; and

that the issuance of $5,000 in Class B Units (non-voting, no dividends, no liquidation preference) to Martin Grasser is hereby authorized and approved; and

that the mark-to-model valuation of the Contributed IP at $10,005,000, without independent third-party appraisal, is hereby acknowledged and approved, and shall be disclosed on the face of all documentation relating to Structure 001; and

that the self-dealing nature of this arrangement is hereby acknowledged and approved by the Board, and shall be disclosed on the face of all documentation relating to Structure 001, including without limitation the board resolution, IP Contribution Agreement, Promissory Note, press release, structure brief, and Class B Unit certificate; and

that Structure 001 shall be consolidated for all accounting and reporting purposes, with no off-balance-sheet treatment; and

that the Corporate Secretary is hereby directed to: (i) record this resolution in the Structure Index as Structure 001, status Execution; (ii) update the Cap Table to reflect the issuance of Class B Units and the Promissory Note to Martin Grasser (affiliated: CEO); and (iii) archive the complete documentation package for Structure 001 in the corporate record; and

that the CEO is authorized and directed to execute all agreements, certificates, filings, and instruments necessary or appropriate to complete Structure 001, subject to review by securities counsel prior to any filing or publication.

RES-2026-005
Authorization of Structure 002 — verse.works Distribution Agreement

the Board has considered the engagement of verse.works as exclusive distribution partner for the Company’s structures, operating under the gallery model; and

verse.works will provide the following services: (i) promotional reach to collectors and participants; (ii) collector access and audience development; and (iii) strategic counsel in connection with the Company’s operations, exhibition activities, and participant engagement; and

the engagement operates under a revenue share structure: verse.works receives a commission on proceeds from the sale of structures; no upfront fees and no fixed payments; verse.works earns when the Company earns; and

the Distribution & Strategic Advisory Agreement (DSA-002) is itself securitized as the primary asset of Protected Series 002 — an uncapitalized series whose sole asset is the contractual right to future distribution revenue, valued at $500,000 under the Company’s mark-to-model methodology; and

verse.works holds, and shall hold, no governance authority over the Company, no voting rights with respect to any class of the Company’s securities, and no equity interest in the Company or in any Protected Series thereof; and

the verse.works distribution relationship shall not be consolidated for accounting or reporting purposes, verse.works being an independent third party; any revenue share payments shall be recorded as contingent liabilities of Protected Series 002; and

the Board has reviewed the risk factors and mitigant disclosures set forth in the Risk-Mitigant Disclosure Schedule attached hereto as Exhibit A, including without limitation items 6 and 7 thereof; and

the Board has determined that the engagement of verse.works as an independent distribution partner is consistent with the Company’s strategic objectives and the Structural Fiduciary, and that the terms and scope of the distribution relationship are adequately disclosed;

that the engagement of verse.works as exclusive distribution partner for the Company’s structures is hereby authorized and approved; and

that the formation of Protected Series 002 under the Company’s Delaware Series LLC, as an uncapitalized series whose sole asset is the Distribution & Strategic Advisory Agreement (DSA-002-2026), is hereby authorized and approved; and

that the CEO is authorized to negotiate and execute the Distribution & Strategic Advisory Agreement (DSA-002-2026) with verse.works, on terms consistent with those described herein; and

that the Agreement shall include, at minimum, the following provisions: (i) verse.works holds no governance authority; (ii) verse.works holds no equity or voting interest in the Company or in any Protected Series; (iii) the distribution relationship is not consolidated; (iv) revenue share is contingent on proceeds and carries no guaranteed minimum; and (v) the nature of the relationship is accurately described in all public-facing materials; and

that the mark-to-model valuation of the Distribution Agreement at $500,000, without independent third-party appraisal, is hereby acknowledged and approved; and

that all public references to verse.works in Company materials, press releases, filings, and participant disclosures shall accurately describe the distribution and promotional nature of the relationship and shall not overstate the scope of verse.works’ role, authority, or involvement in governance; and

that the Corporate Secretary is hereby directed to: (i) record this resolution in the Structure Index as Structure 002, status Proposed; (ii) note verse.works as an unaffiliated independent counterparty; and (iii) archive the executed Distribution Agreement in the corporate record upon completion; and

that the CEO is authorized and directed to execute all agreements, certificates, and instruments necessary or appropriate to complete Structure 002, subject to review by securities counsel prior to any filing or publication.

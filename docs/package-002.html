Distribution & Strategic Advisory Agreement

DSA-002-2026

This Distribution & Strategic Advisory Agreement (this “Agreement”) is entered into as of [Date], by and between:

Party A: Capital Structures, Inc., a Delaware C-Corporation, acting through Protected Series 002 (the “Company” or “Series 002”)

Party B: verse.works (the “Advisor” or “Distribution Partner”)

WHEREAS, the Company’s sole product is the creation, execution, and disclosure of corporate and financial structures;

WHEREAS, the Company seeks to engage verse.works as its exclusive distribution partner under the gallery model: the Company creates the structures, verse.works provides promotional reach, collector access, and strategic positioning;

WHEREAS, the Distribution Agreement is itself securitized as the primary asset of Protected Series 002 — an uncapitalized series whose sole asset is the contractual right to future distribution revenue;

WHEREAS, the Company operates under the Structural Fiduciary, which is disclosed to all participants as a material term of participation;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants set forth herein, the parties agree as follows:

Section 1. Engagement

The Company hereby engages verse.works as its exclusive distribution partner for the promotion, positioning, and sale of the Company’s structures, including Class B Units, licensing arrangements, and any other monetization events attributable to the Company’s operations.

Section 2. Services

verse.works shall provide the following services:

  • Promotional reach within the contemporary art, institutional design, and collector communities
  • Collector access and relationship management
  • Strategic counsel on positioning, presentation, and market development

verse.works does not engage in broker-dealer activity. All securities transactions shall occur through registered Regulation Crowdfunding portals in compliance with applicable securities laws.

Section 3. Revenue Share

In consideration of the services described herein, the Company shall pay verse.works a revenue share as follows:

  • Trigger Events: Proceeds from the sale of Class B Units, licensing of structures, or any monetization event attributable to Company structures
  • Commission: [__]% of net proceeds (to be negotiated)
  • Payment: Within 30 days of receipt of proceeds by the Company
  • Exclusivity: verse.works is the exclusive distribution partner for the term of this Agreement
  • Term: Co-terminus with the Company or as mutually agreed

The revenue share is contingent on the receipt of proceeds. If no proceeds are received, no payment is owed. verse.works bears the same timing risk as the Company.

Section 4. What verse.works Does Not Receive

  • No equity in any series or the parent
  • No governance authority or voting rights
  • No fixed fees or guaranteed payments
  • No claim senior to PN-001 in dissolution
  • No broker-dealer activity — verse.works promotes and positions; all securities transactions occur through registered Reg CF portals

Section 5. Valuation

This Agreement is valued at $500,000 under the Company’s mark-to-model methodology. The model reflects: (i) the anticipated value of verse.works’ promotional reach and collector network applied to the Company’s Reg CF offering and future structure sales; (ii) the strategic value of verse.works’ positioning within the contemporary art and institutional design community; and (iii) the projected revenue share over the anticipated life of this Agreement. No independent appraisal has been obtained.

Section 6. Accounting Treatment

Series 002 is uncapitalized. No cash contribution has been made. This Agreement is carried as an intangible asset at its mark-to-model value of $500,000. The revenue share obligation is contingent — recorded at $0 until proceeds are received. When proceeds flow, the Company records the revenue share payable to verse.works as an operating liability of Series 002. The Series may carry negative equity when commissions exceed the nominal asset value. This is anticipated and disclosed.

Section 7. Consolidation

Fully consolidated into Capital Structures, Inc. financial statements. verse.works is an independent third party and is not consolidated. This Agreement is an asset of Series 002; the revenue share payable to verse.works is a contingent liability of Series 002.

Section 8. Representations and Disclosures

8.1 Self-Dealing Disclosure. Martin Grasser, as CEO and sole director, approves the engagement of verse.works and the terms of this Agreement. verse.works is an independent third party with no affiliated relationship to the Company’s officers. This arrangement is disclosed.

8.2 Structural Fiduciary. verse.works acknowledges that the Company operates under the Structural Fiduciary, which establishes that management decisions may include forming entities with no revenue-generating purpose, entering economically neutral transactions, maintaining excess complexity, and dissolving the Company at a moment determined to be structurally complete. The Doctrine is a material term of this engagement.

Section 9. Term

This Agreement shall remain in effect until the earliest to occur of: (i) dissolution of the Company; (ii) dissolution of Protected Series 002; (iii) mutual written agreement of the parties; or (iv) material breach by either party, subject to 30 days’ written notice and opportunity to cure.

Section 10. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflicts of law principles.

Structural Fiduciary

Foundational Governance Document · Capital Structures, Inc.

Preamble

Capital Structures, Inc. (the “Company”) is a Delaware C-Corporation whose sole product is the creation, execution, and disclosure of corporate and financial structures. The Company does not sell goods or services. It publishes structures.

The Board of Directors has adopted this Structural Fiduciary (the “Doctrine”) as a foundational governance document establishing the principles under which the Company’s fiduciary obligations are to be interpreted and discharged.

Operative Provisions

The Board’s fiduciary obligations include:

  1. Truthful Disclosure. All material facts, transactions, self-dealing arrangements, and risks shall be disclosed fully and promptly. The Company does not conceal information from participants, holders, counterparties, or the public.
  2. Procedural Integrity. All corporate actions shall be properly authorized, documented, and recorded. The Company maintains a complete corporate record and follows its own governance procedures.
  3. Structural Good Faith. Management decisions shall be made in good faith toward the realization of the Company’s purpose as defined in its charter — the creation, execution, and disclosure of corporate and financial structures as a published form.

Permitted Actions

Under this Doctrine, management decisions may include:

  • Forming entities with no revenue-generating purpose
  • Entering economically neutral transactions
  • Maintaining excess complexity
  • Dissolving the Company at a moment determined to be structurally complete

Such actions, when taken in good faith and with full disclosure, are consistent with the Board’s fiduciary duties under this Doctrine.

Disclosure

This Doctrine shall be disclosed to all participants, holders, and counterparties as a material term of participation in any Company offering. Acknowledgment of the Doctrine is required in all subscription agreements, contribution agreements, and advisory agreements.

Risk Factors

Capital Structures, Inc. · Protected Series 002

The following risk factors should be considered carefully before entering into any agreement or transaction involving Capital Structures, Inc. or Protected Series 002. This list is not exhaustive.

Principal Risk Factors

  1. No Revenue. The Company may never generate revenue. The Company’s sole product is the creation, execution, and disclosure of corporate and financial structures. There is no other revenue source, no goods, and no services.
  2. Intentional Dissolution. The Company may intentionally dissolve at any time. Dissolution is a valid completion of the work under the Structural Fiduciary. The Board is not obligated to maintain the Company’s existence.
  3. Management Not Incentivized. Management is not incentivized to increase the value of any securities. CEO compensation is not tied to stock price or company performance. Management prioritizes structural integrity and conceptual coherence over profit maximization.
  4. No Resale Market. Class B Units may have no resale market. No public market exists or is expected to exist for Class B Units. Units are subject to Company transfer restrictions.
  5. Expect Total Loss. Participants should expect total loss of their contribution. The Company makes no representation that any participant will recover any portion of their investment.

Additional Risk Factors

Self-Dealing

Martin Grasser acts simultaneously as sole director, CEO, and holder of Class B Units in Protected Series 001. He approves his own transactions, sets his own compensation, and controls all governance decisions. He also approves the terms of the Distribution Agreement with verse.works in his capacity as sole director. This arrangement is intentional and disclosed.

Mark-to-Model Valuation

All asset valuations are based on the Company’s internal mark-to-model methodology. No independent third-party appraisal has been obtained. The Distribution Agreement (DSA-002) is valued at $500,000, which may bear no relationship to any realizable value.

Contingent Revenue Share

The revenue share payable to verse.works under DSA-002 is entirely contingent on the receipt of proceeds by the Company. The Company may never generate revenue, may never sell any Class B Units, and may intentionally dissolve before any monetization event occurs. verse.works may never receive any payment under this Agreement.

No Guaranteed Distribution Activity

While verse.works is engaged as the Company’s exclusive distribution partner, the Agreement does not guarantee any minimum level of distribution activity, promotional effort, or collector engagement. The Company has no contractual remedy if verse.works’ efforts do not result in sales.

AI Agent Dependency

The Company uses AI agents to generate corporate documentation. All agent outputs require human review, but the Company’s operational capacity depends in part on the continued availability and performance of these systems.

No Independent Appraisal

No independent third-party appraisal of the Distribution Agreement, the Contributed IP, or any other Company asset has been obtained. All valuations are internally generated.

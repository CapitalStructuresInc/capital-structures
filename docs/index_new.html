How to Participate

Participants capitalize the parent company. You are not buying a separate art project. You are buying a position inside a real corporation whose public administration is the artwork.

1
The Company lists on a Reg CF portal. Anyone can participate.
2
Your capital enters the parent company’s treasury.
3
You appear on the Participant Ledger — a public record.
4
You receive parent-level securities. Real securities. No voting rights, no dividends, no liquidation preference.
5
The board deploys treasury into operations, contracts, reserves, and purpose structures. You watch it happen.

No securities are currently being offered. The offering will be conducted through a Reg CF portal in compliance with applicable securities laws.

What the Board Does With Capital

The board does not only create entities. It has a full ladder of deployment tools. Most board actions are not entity formation. They are operating spend, contracts, reserves, and policy.

Purpose structures are formed only when the company needs a new boundary around ownership, liability, cash flow, or reporting. If deleting the structure changes only the poetry, it should not be a legal entity.

Failure Is Part of the Work

The company may allow individual structures to fail economically, but not invisibly. A structure is allowed to fail. The failure must become document, ledger, and diagram.

The Company may never generate profit and may intentionally dissolve at a moment determined to be structurally complete.

NASDAQ

The Board has adopted a long-term objective of listing the Company’s securities on a national securities exchange. The preferred path is reverse acquisition of an existing publicly listed company.

No timeline has been adopted. The Company may pursue it actively, passively, opportunistically, or not at all.

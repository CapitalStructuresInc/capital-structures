# Capital Structures, Inc. — Public-Facing Voice + Site Direction

## Hero options

1. **Participation capitalizes the work.**
2. **Public capital is the medium.**
3. **The company is the artwork.**
4. **The annual report is the exhibition.**

## Recommended hero

**Participation capitalizes the work.**

## Recommended subhead

Capital Structures, Inc. is an art operating company that uses public participation to originate, administer, report, and publish itself as a visible institution.

## Short public description

Capital Structures, Inc. is a single public-facing art operating company. Participants buy into the parent company. The company uses that capital to maintain its archive, publish its reports, stage its operations, and form internal structures only when they change something real about ownership, cash flow, risk, or reporting.

## Plain-language explanation

You are not buying a separate art project.
You are participating in one company whose product is its own visible administration.

## Tone rules

- cold, formal, institutional
- never cute
- never over-explained
- documentary first, uncanny second
- let corporate language carry the charge
- reveal ambition through structure, not hype

## Website architecture

### Main pages
- Home
- Architecture
- Annual Report
- Archive
- Ledger
- Research
- Glossary

### Home should do four things
1. establish the company as the artwork
2. explain that participants capitalize the parent
3. show that treasury can form structure
4. point toward archive and annual report

### Architecture page should do four things
1. explain parent issuer / treasury / reporting / optional vehicles
2. explain why not every participant gets a separate vehicle
3. define purpose vehicles as backstage operating instruments
4. provide the master company diagram

### Annual Report page
This should feel like the central public object.
Potential sections:
- Letter from the CEO
- Statement of operations
- Treasury and deployments
- Vehicle activity
- Archive highlights
- Research notes
- Risk and disclosure notes
- Corporate portrait

### Archive page
This should be the institutional memory of the work.
Not everything needs to be public immediately, but the public page should imply a serious document history.

## Lines worth using

- **The company is the artwork.**
- **Participants capitalize the institution.**
- **Treasury becomes structure.**
- **The annual report is the principal public release.**
- **Operations, reporting, and archive are among the company’s products.**
- **Internal vehicles are formed only when they change something real.**
- **The public does not fund separate projects. The public capitalizes one visible institution.**

## What to retire

- “the company’s only asset is belief”
- “each collector gets a structure”
- “six types cycling” as the core public experience
- anything that makes the company feel merely ironic or decorative

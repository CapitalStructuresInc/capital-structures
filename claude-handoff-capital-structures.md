## Executive summary

Capital Structures, Inc. is **one public-facing art operating company**.
Participants capitalize the parent company.
The parent company holds treasury.
The board uses treasury to operate the institution, publish its public life, maintain its archive, and only when justified form internal purpose structures.

But the project must still be read through its original risk lens:
- risk does not disappear
- exposure is conserved
- formal legal separation is not the same as aggregate exposure
- a structure should change picture under unconsolidated vs. consolidated views
- a structure should change picture under base case vs. trigger state

The best version of the project is:

**one institution + one treasury + one archive + one public life**

combined with

**a sharp method for reading structures under multiple conditions**.

## What must not be lost

These ideas are essential and must be restored or preserved:

1. **Risk does not disappear.**
   A structure does not abolish exposure. It determines where exposure appears, under what name, at what cost, with what recourse, and at what distance from the originating entity.

2. **Exposure is conserved.**
   Risk displaced from one layer of a structure reappears elsewhere.

3. **Disclosure is not transparency.**
   A structure may be fully documented and still remain difficult to see.

4. **The diagram is an argument.**
   Every arrow, guarantee, exclusion, reserve, and trigger path is part of the disclosed arrangement.

5. **Unconsolidated vs. consolidated matters.**
   The clean entity story is not the same thing as the aggregate exposure story.

6. **Base case vs. trigger state matters.**
   A structure at rest is not the same thing as a structure under pressure.

7. **Residual exposure matters.**
   Every isolation mechanism leaves something behind.

8. **If neither picture changes, it is not yet a structure. It is only a document.**

## Core architecture

### 1. Parent issuer — Capital Structures, Inc.

This is the only public issuer.

Functions:
- raises capital through the regulated public entry point
- maintains the participant ledger / cap table
- holds treasury
- signs contracts
- commissions design, engineering, legal, accounting, archive, and presentation work
- controls the website, archive, public voice, and reporting cadence
- makes board-approved allocations into operations, reserves, and purpose structures
- publishes required reporting and selected project-facing materials

This is the public institution and the public artwork.

### 2. Participation security

Participants buy securities of the parent issuer.

Working concept:
- one public participation class at the parent level
- clear rights package and restrictions
- designed so the public is capitalizing the parent, not buying a new downstream vehicle each time

The point is not that the security becomes less real because the project is art.
The point is that the security is one of the project’s actual materials.

### 3. Treasury / formation reserve

Treasury is the central pool of participant capital at the parent.

Treasury is used to:
- keep the company alive
- fund operations and public presentation
- build and maintain the archive
- produce documents, diagrams, interfaces, and reports
- support exhibitions, launches, and public programs
- create reserves and consequence buffers
- capitalize internal purpose structures when they are justified

Participants are not merely listed.
They are booked.

### 4. Public administration layer

This is the visible spine of the work.

Functions:
- participant ledger (in selected or redacted form)
- architecture page
- treasury deployment summaries
- board actions and resolutions (selected)
- archive and operating history
- annual reports
- diagrams and schedules
- institutional correspondence and public-facing documents where appropriate

### 5. Operations / servicing / presentation

This is where verse.works fits best at the start.

Role of verse.works:
- platform / gallery / presentation partner
- servicing and communications support
- staging of the company as public institution
- support for launches, presentations, documentation, and possibly registry-facing experiences

Important design choice:
- this begins as a **contractual role**, not a co-equal public issuer
- verse.works does not need to become a separate vehicle in the first build

### 6. IP / rights / archive layer

The company’s IP, marks, archive assets, source files, diagrams, visual identity, and report materials may initially sit at the parent.

A separate rights vehicle should be formed only if there is a real reason to do so:
- cleaner ownership separation
- licensing program
- archive custody
- succession / stewardship planning
- risk or liability management

## The board deployment toolkit

The board does **not** only create vehicles.
It has a full ladder of deployment tools.

### Tool 1 — Operating spend
Use treasury directly for:
- design
- engineering
- legal
- accounting
- archive work
- editorial work
- public presentation
- website and interface maintenance
- documentation production

### Tool 2 — Contracts
Use agreements when the company needs a counterparty rather than a new entity.
Examples:
- servicing agreement with verse.works
- design and engineering engagement
- exhibition agreement
- licensing agreement
- archive storage / custody agreement

### Tool 3 — Board-designated reserves
Use policy and internal accounting designations when risk needs to be acknowledged without creating a new vehicle.
Examples:
- contingency reserve
- reporting reserve
- exhibition reserve
- archive reserve

### Tool 4 — Schedules / policies / internal ledgers
Use structured documentation when the company needs definition rather than separation.
Examples:
- treasury deployment policy
- trigger schedule
- archive policy
- servicing standards
- rights schedule
- reserve policy

### Tool 5 — Purpose structures
Use a vehicle only when the company truly needs a new asset boundary, liability boundary, servicing boundary, reserve boundary, or reporting boundary.

Critical rule:

**If the board can solve the problem with a policy, contract, reserve, or schedule, it should not form a vehicle just for atmosphere.**

## What the purpose structures actually do

Purpose structures are **backstage operating instruments**.
They are not separate public art projects.
They are not automatically created for each participant.
They are not primarily self-referential.

They are justified only when deleting them would change at least one of these:
- who owns an asset
- who receives cash
- who bears liability
- who services the public
- what happens under stress
- what has to be reported separately

If deleting the structure changes only the poetry, it should not be a legal entity.
It should be a page, schedule, reserve, or diagram layer.

### Purpose vehicle taxonomy

#### A. IP / Archive HoldCo
**What it does:**
- holds core IP, marks, source files, archive materials, visual identity, and possibly report materials

**Why the board would form it:**
- to separate core assets from day-to-day operating risk
- to enable cleaner licensing
- to formalize custody and continuity

**What it is doing for the company:**
- protecting the long-term estate of the work
- making authorship and ownership legible
- giving the institution a durable backbone

#### B. Licensing / Partnerships Vehicle
**What it does:**
- enters a specific licensing, publishing, institutional, or commercial partnership program

**Why the board would form it:**
- if partnership revenues, obligations, approvals, or liabilities need their own paper

**What it is doing for the company:**
- converting the company’s IP and public identity into a managed revenue-bearing program

#### C. Exhibition / Production Vehicle
**What it does:**
- holds venue contracts, fabrication expenses, staffing, shipping, insurance, and exhibition-specific liabilities

**Why the board would form it:**
- if an exhibition becomes large enough to justify separate accounting or liability containment

**What it is doing for the company:**
- allowing public presentation to scale without making every exhibition a direct parent-level exposure

#### D. Reserve / Support Vehicle
**What it does:**
- holds ring-fenced reserves, support pools, contingency cash, or guarantee capacity

**Why the board would form it:**
- if a reserve must be meaningfully separated from general treasury
- if a counterparty, venue, or program requires visible support backing

**What it is doing for the company:**
- turning consequence management into a visible structure
- giving the company a formal way to handle pressure, delay, or failure

#### E. Archive / Stewardship Vehicle
**What it does:**
- holds long-term custody responsibilities for the archive, reporting history, and institutional memory

**Why the board would form it:**
- if the archive becomes large, valuable, historically important, or needs continuity beyond ordinary operations

**What it is doing for the company:**
- separating the institution’s memory from the turbulence of current operations
- making stewardship part of the work

#### F. Distribution / Servicing Vehicle
**What it does:**
- administers registry-facing experiences, participant communications, public servicing, or operational support systems

**Why the board would form it:**
- only if servicing grows beyond what should remain a direct parent contract

**What it is doing for the company:**
- formalizing the company’s interface with the public
- turning service into a managed operating layer

## Are the structures self-referential?

Sometimes, but they should not be **only** self-referential.

The right answer is:
- some structures are asset boundaries
- some are liability boundaries
- some are servicing boundaries
- some are reporting boundaries
- some are reserve boundaries
- and only some are deliberately reflective or recursive

A good structure does not merely point back at the company.
It changes how the company owns, spends, contracts, supports, or reports.

### Add a Method / Risk Lens block near the top of the architecture page

Use this copy:

> Capital Structures, Inc. is a single public institution, but its structures are still read in multiple conditions.
>
> On an **unconsolidated** basis, a structure appears according to its formal legal boundaries, stated obligations, and authorized separations.
>
> On a **consolidated** basis, the same arrangement is read across guarantees, dependencies, contingent support, servicing needs, and consequence paths that persist beyond those boundaries.
>
> Where available, each structure is also shown in two states.
>
> **Base Case** records the arrangement as authorized, funded, and disclosed. Triggers are dormant.
>
> **Trigger State** records what activates when a shortfall, guarantee event, liquidity event, maturity event, dissolution event, counterparty failure, governance action, or other contractual condition causes the diagram to move.
>
> These are not alternate interpretations. They are alternate tests of the same structure.
>
> A published structure should look cleaner on an unconsolidated basis than on a consolidated basis, and calmer in base case than in trigger state. If neither picture changes, it is not yet a structure. It is only a document.

## Expand the archive entries

Each structure card or section should include:
- Summary
- Unconsolidated
- Consolidated
- Base Case
- Trigger State
- Balance Sheet
- Residual Exposure
- Documents

### Structure 001 — use this as the model

> **Structure 001 — IP Contribution & Pre-Pay Architecture**  
> Authorized · Formation
>
> The Company’s foundational structure. Martin Grasser contributes all intellectual property to Protected Series 001 via ICA-001. In return, Series 001 issues a $10,000,000 Promissory Note and $1,000 in Class B Units. The structure is 99.99% debt-financed. Self-dealing is disclosed, documented, and intentional.
>
> **Unconsolidated**  
> Protected Series 001 acquires contributed intellectual property under ICA-001. Consideration consists of PN-001 and $1,000 in Class B Units. Recourse is limited to Series assets except as expressly supported by the parent.
>
> **Consolidated**  
> The same arrangement reunites valuation risk, founder compensation, governance discretion, and support expectation across the parent and Series 001. The legal boundary remains intact. The exposure boundary does not.
>
> **Base Case**  
> The IP is booked at mark-to-model. The note remains contingent. The series appears discrete, authorized, and sufficiently documented.
>
> **Trigger State**  
> A liquidity event, public listing, dissolution, mutual agreement, or stated maturity converts contingent structure into payable consequence. Support expectations migrate toward the parent. The diagram begins to move.
>
> **Balance Sheet**  
> - Assets: Contributed IP — $10,001,000 (mark-to-model)  
> - Liabilities: Promissory Note — $10,000,000  
> - Equity: Class B Units — $1,000
>
> **Residual Exposure**  
> Role overlap, mark-to-model valuation, no independent appraisal, and effective dependence on the parent despite formal separation. The structure is 99.99% debt-financed. That ratio is part of the disclosed arrangement, not an accident.
>
> **Documents**  
> - IP Contribution Agreement (ICA-001-2026)  
> - Promissory Note (PN-001-2026)  
> - Board Resolution (BOD-2026-001)

### Structure 002 — use this as the servicing model

> **Structure 002 — verse.works Distribution Agreement**  
> Proposed · Servicing
>
> A proposed servicing structure through which verse.works acts as exclusive distribution partner under the gallery model. Contingent revenue share. No fixed fees. Sole asset: the contractual right to future distribution revenue.
>
> **Unconsolidated**  
> Protected Series 002 holds the distribution agreement as its sole asset. verse.works is compensated only through contingent revenue share tied to monetization events. No fixed fee is owed. No equity is granted. The series is uncapitalized.
>
> **Consolidated**  
> The Company’s distribution infrastructure sits outside the operating company but remains necessary to promotion, participant access, and revenue realization. Separation persists. Dependency persists with it.
>
> **Base Case**  
> No sale, license, or offering proceeds. No commission accrues. The series remains structurally live but economically dormant.
>
> **Trigger State**  
> Any monetization event activates the revenue-share waterfall. Because the series is uncapitalized, timing mismatch, negative equity, and dependence on external servicing become visible immediately.
>
> **Balance Sheet**  
> - Assets: Distribution Agreement — mark-to-model  
> - Liabilities: Revenue Share Payable — contingent  
> - Equity: $0 — uncapitalized
>
> **Residual Exposure**  
> Go-to-market dependence on an independent third party, no binding performance obligation, and the possibility that monetization activates obligations faster than the series can support them.
>
> **Documents**  
> - Distribution & Strategic Advisory Agreement (DSA-002-2026)  
> - Board Resolution  
> - Revenue Share Schedule

## Glossary additions that must come back

Add or restore these terms:

### Unconsolidated
What the structure is permitted to say about itself. The view bounded by formal legal separations, stated recourse, and authorized entity lines.

What the structure looks like when guarantees, dependencies, support expectations, and consequence paths are allowed to speak across those boundaries.

### Base Case
Authorized, funded, disclosed. Triggers dormant.

### Trigger State
Contingent conditions active. The diagram is in motion.

### Residual Exposure
Risk that remains after transfer, isolation, or ring-fencing. What the structure cannot fully exile.

### Exposure Conservation
The proposition that risk displaced from one layer of a structure must reappear elsewhere rather than disappear.

### Capital Structures usage
A doctrine of structural thermodynamics.

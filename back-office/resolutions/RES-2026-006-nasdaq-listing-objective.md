# CAPITAL STRUCTURES INC.
## Board Resolution — RES-2026-006

### RESOLUTION ADOPTING LONG-TERM LISTING OBJECTIVE

**Proposed by:** Martin Grasser, Chief Executive Officer
**Status:** Proposed

### WHEREAS

1. Capital Structures, Inc. (the "Company") is a Delaware C-Corporation whose sole product is the creation, execution, and disclosure of corporate and financial structures;

2. The Company's charter establishes the Structural Fiduciary standard, under which management decisions may include forming entities with no revenue-generating purpose, entering economically neutral transactions, maintaining excess complexity, and dissolving the Company at a moment determined to be structurally complete;

3. The Board believes that a listing on a national securities exchange would constitute a significant structural event in the life of the Company — not because it would increase the value of any security, but because it would subject the Company's operations, disclosures, and governance to the full reporting and compliance apparatus of a publicly traded corporation;

4. The Board has considered multiple paths to a public listing, including but not limited to: direct listing, traditional IPO, SPAC merger, and reverse acquisition of an existing publicly listed company;

5. The Board believes that a **reverse acquisition** of an existing NASDAQ-listed company represents the most structurally interesting and operationally feasible path to a national exchange listing, as the transaction itself would constitute a disclosed corporate structure of significant complexity — involving valuation, negotiation, regulatory filing, shareholder approval, and public disclosure at a scale the Company has not yet operated;

### NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED

1. **Adoption of Listing Objective.** The Board hereby adopts, as a long-term corporate objective, the listing of the Company's securities on a national securities exchange, with the NASDAQ Stock Market as the preferred venue.

2. **Preferred Path.** The Board identifies reverse acquisition of an existing publicly listed company as the preferred structural path to achieving the listing objective. The Board notes that candidate companies may include those currently trading below book value, facing delisting proceedings, or operating in advanced financial distress — conditions that create the structural conditions for a feasible transaction.

3. **No Timeline.** The Board does not adopt a timeline for achieving the listing objective. The objective is stated as a structural aspiration, not a performance target. The Company may pursue it actively, passively, opportunistically, or not at all.

4. **No Obligation to Maximize.** Consistent with the Structural Fiduciary, the adoption of a listing objective does not create any obligation to maximize share price, pursue profitable operations, or take any action primarily intended to increase the market value of the Company's securities. The listing objective exists because the listing itself is a structure.

5. **Disclosure.** This resolution shall be published in full on the Company's website and archived in the Company's governance records.

6. **Forward-Looking Statement.** The Board acknowledges that the listing objective constitutes a forward-looking statement subject to significant uncertainty. The Company may never achieve a public listing. The Company may intentionally abandon the objective at any time. Past corporate actions are not indicative of future corporate actions.

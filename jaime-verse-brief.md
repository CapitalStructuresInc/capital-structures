# Capital Structures, Inc. — Jaime / verse.works Brief

## What this is

Capital Structures, Inc. is one public-facing art operating company.
It is not a studio wrapper for separate projects.
The company itself is the artwork.

Participants buy into the parent company.
The parent company holds treasury.
The board uses that capital to operate the institution and, only when justified, form internal purpose vehicles.

## What verse.works is being asked to support

verse.works is not being asked to become a co-issuer or securities portal.
At the start, verse.works is best understood as a presentation, servicing, and public-interface partner.

Possible roles:
- gallery / staging partner
- public presentation partner
- launch and communications partner
- archive and document-display partner
- participant-facing interface partner
- exhibition / report-release host

## The public artwork

The public artwork is the visible administration of the company:
- website
- archive
- diagrams
- governance materials
- selected filings
- annual report

The annual report is the flagship recurring public object.
It functions as report, exhibition catalog, management discussion, and self-portrait of the institution.

## Why this is cleaner

Old version:
- every buyer triggered a new structure
- the public face was six cycling structure types
- the company risked feeling like a shell producing clever financial jokes

New version:
- one parent issuer
- one participant base
- one treasury
- one archive
- one annual report
- vehicles formed only when they serve a real operating function

## The simplest way to explain it

Capital Structures, Inc. is an art operating company whose medium is its own public administration.
Participants capitalize the company.
The company publishes and stages its own operations.

## What stays backstage

Internal vehicles, reserve structures, legal drafts, and support agreements can remain mostly backstage unless they improve public understanding.

## What stays frontstage

The parent institution, the annual report, the archive, and selected diagrams remain frontstage.

## One-line version

**The company is the artwork, and the annual report is its principal public release.**
